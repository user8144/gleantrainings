01Garbage in, garbage out

Why the same question can give different answers

Ask, "What's our current PTO policy?" and you might get either response below. A co-worker might get something else. The difference comes down to the source material Glean references, and it may not always be correct.

"What's our current PTO policy?"
Accrue a capped amount of PTO each month and submit requests through BambooHR…
source: Current PTO Guidelines (2021)
"What's our current PTO policy?"
Full-time employees get flexible, unlimited PTO; submit in Workday for manager approval.
source: US Paid Time Off (PTO) Policy
When an answer is weak, it's almost always one of these things:
ASource problem

The doc it used is stale, wrong, or not the source of truth.

BPrompt problem

The question was too broad, so it grounded on the wrong thing.

CKnowledge gap or access problem

Sometimes it's neither; you could ask a question in which a solid answer doesn't exist or you don't have access to where the answer exists, so Glean makes something up.

◎

The takeaway: Glean is only as good as the prompts you give it and the sources it references. This module shares how you can improve both for better results.

02Prompting guidance

Clear is kind

Think of your files as a haystack. With each layer of detail you give in your prompt, the smaller the haystack Glean has to search for your needle.

03Sourcing guidance

Beware: a confident answer isn't a verified one.

Glean's response will use its highest confidence matches to your query, but that doesn't mean its answers will be correct.

Your confidence comes from understanding what sources your chat references, or asking it to cite its sources if it doesn't.

?

The trust test: "Would I repeat this, route it to a customer, or operationalize it without checking the source?" If not, open the citation first.
