Lesson 4

Trust habits that improve outcomes

Good results are not just about asking better questions. They also depend on verifying what kind of answer you actually got.

When to verify

When precision matters policy, process, approvals, dates, requirements, or source-of-truth questions.
When the answer feels too generic this often signals weak grounding or weak scoping.
When multiple docs could apply duplicates and outdated content are a cue to inspect the source set.

What to do when the answer is off

Weak prompt add task, context, scope, and output shape.
Weak grounding use @ to anchor the answer to the right resource.
Wrong workflow route back to Search if you need to identify the real source first.

A practical habit loop

Ask → inspect the sources → decide whether the issue is prompt quality, grounding, or source quality → retry with a more intentional move.
