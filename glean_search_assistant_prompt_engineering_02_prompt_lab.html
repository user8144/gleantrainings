Lesson 2

Prompt lab

Most prompt improvement comes from adding a few missing ingredients, not from sounding clever.

A simple formula

Task What do you want Glean to do?
Context What team, process, topic, or use case matters?
Scope What sources, time period, or boundaries apply?
Output What shape should the answer take?

Vague vs stronger

Too vague

Tell me about our enablement process.

Stronger

Summarize Cribl's enablement process for new internal training launches. Focus on the roles involved, expected inputs, and common handoff points. Give me a 5-bullet summary and cite the main source docs.

Prompt rewrite exercise

Weak prompt: What should I do?

Why it fails: No task, no context, no scope, no output shape.

Rewrite:

I'm preparing a quick internal update about a KM initiative. Based on current Cribl documentation, give me a short summary of the initiative, the intended audience, and the next milestone. Keep it to 6 bullets and point me to the source docs I should double-check before sending.

What to add when results are weak

Add the audience “for a GTM manager,” “for a new employee,” “for content owners.”
Add the job to be done summarize, compare, extract decisions, draft, or locate.
Add source expectations ask for citations, key docs, or route to the underlying materials.
Add grounding use @ when a specific doc, Collection, or resource should drive the answer.
